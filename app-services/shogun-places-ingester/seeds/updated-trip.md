Step-by-Step Walking Directions
1. Ginkakuji (The Silver Pavilion) (Start) 
•	Begin here in the morning (opens 8:30 AM) to avoid crowds. Tour the silver pavilion and its dry sand garden.
•	Exit: Head toward the small canal nearby. 
2. Philosopher's Path (approx. 20–30 min walk) 
•	Follow the stone path southward along the cherry tree-lined canal.
•	Tip: Veer off to visit smaller, quiet temples along the way, such as Honen-in. 
 Japan National Tourism Organization +2
3. Nanzenji Temple Area (20 min walk from path end)
•	The path ends near Eikando Temple; continue walking south to reach the large complex of Nanzenji Temple.
•	Highlights: Explore the massive Sanmon gate and the brick Meiji-period aqueduct. 
4. Heian Shrine (15–20 min walk from Nanzenji)
•	Walk west from Nanzenji toward the Okazaki area.
•	Highlight: View the massive red torii gate and stroll through the shrine gardens. 
5. Maruyama Park & Yasaka Shrine (15 min walk from Heian)
•	Head south towards the Gion area to enter Maruyama Park, known for its weeping cherry tree.
•	Highlight: Walk through the park directly into Yasaka Shrine, the center of Gion. 
 6. Higashiyama Historic Streets (15 min walk from Yasaka) 
•	Exit the back of Yasaka shrine and walk south toward Kodaiji Temple (optional stop).
•	Follow the signs for Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka streets. These are traditional pedestrian streets with shops and cafes. 
7. Kiyomizudera Temple (10–15 min uphill from streets) 
•	Follow Sannenzaka uphill (south) to the entrance of Kiyomizudera.
•	Highlight: Watch the sunset over Kyoto from the iconic wooden stage. 
Key Tips for the Trip
•	Best Time to Start: Start at 8:00 AM or 8:30 AM at Ginkakuji.
•	Best Direction: North-to-South (Ginkakuji 
  Kiyomizudera) is generally downhill or level, except for the final walk up to Kiyomizudera.
•	Crowds: The Philosopher's Path and Higashiyama streets (Ninenzaka/Sannenzaka) are very crowded during peak season (April/November).
•	Transportation: Kyoto City Bus #100 or #5 from Kyoto Station goes to Ginkakuji-mae.
•	Food: Stop for lunch in the Nanzenji area or in the Gion/Higashiyama district before walking up to Kiyomizudera.
