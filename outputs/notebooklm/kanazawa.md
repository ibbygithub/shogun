# Kanazawa — Points of Interest

## Entertainment

### 21st Century Museum of Contemporary Art / 金沢21世紀美術館
📍 1-2-1 Hirosaka, Kanazawa, Ishikawa 920-8509
🏷️ group, entertainment
👥 Crowd: Not typically crowded. Some permanent installations have timed entry (book at desk).
⏰ Best time: Afternoon works well. Famous 'swimming pool' installation by Leandro Erlich — you stand underwater looking up through glass at people standing at the bottom of a drained pool. Free to enter main areas; paid zone ~360 yen.

## Food

### Omicho Market / 近江町市場
📍 50 Kamiomicho, Kanazawa, Ishikawa 920-0906
🏷️ brenda, group, food, seafood
👥 Crowd: Kanazawa is Japan Sea country — exceptional seafood quality, especially winter crab (zuwaigani) and yellowtail (buri/kanburi). Busiest 9-11am.
⏰ Best time: Arrive 9am. Covered market with 170+ stalls. Brenda: outstanding fish and seafood options. Restaurant stalls inside offer sushi and kaisen-don (seafood rice bowls). Fresh crab to go or at seated restaurants.

## Sightseeing

### Higashi Chaya District / 東茶屋街
📍 Higashiyama 1-chome, Kanazawa, Ishikawa
🏷️ group, todd, sightseeing, ceramics
👥 Crowd: Less crowded than Kyoto's equivalent geisha districts. Many Kutani ware ceramics shops on and around the main street.
⏰ Best time: Morning (10am open). The preserved teahouse street is walkable in 30 min. Todd: multiple dedicated Kutani ware (九谷焼) retailers — look for bold overglaze enamel painting on porcelain. Some shops allow watching craftspeople.

### Kenroku-en Garden / 兼六園
📍 1 Kenrokumachi, Kanazawa, Ishikawa 920-0936
🏷️ group, sightseeing, cherry-blossom
👥 Crowd: One of Japan's three great gardens. Cherry blossoms may still be in peak late March-early April window — Kanazawa is slightly later than Osaka.
⏰ Best time: Opens 7am in spring (free before 8am). Pair with Kanazawa Castle Park adjacent (no extra charge). The famous Kotoji lantern on the pond is the most photographed spot.

## Temples & Shrines

**D.T. Suzuki Museum**
Minimalist museum honoring Zen Buddhist philosopher. Beautiful Water Mirror Garden for contemplation. ¥310. Near 21st Century Museum.

**Higashi Chaya District [higashi-chaya]**
Beautifully preserved geisha district with wooden tea houses from 1820s. Gold leaf shops, traditional cafes. Most photogenic area in Kanazawa.

**Kenroku-en Garden [kenrokuen]**
One of Japan's top 3 gardens. Stunning in every season — snow-covered yukitsuri in winter, cherry blossoms spring. ¥320 entry. Allow 1-2 hours.

**Nagamachi Samurai District [nagamachi]**
Preserved samurai residences with earthen walls and water channels. Nomura-ke house ¥550, one of Japan's best samurai homes.

**Nishi Chaya District [nishi-chaya]**
Smaller, quieter geisha quarter on the west side. Fewer tourists. Myoryuji (Ninja Temple) is nearby.
