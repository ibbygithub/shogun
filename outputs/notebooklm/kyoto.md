# Kyoto Itinerary

## Sunday, March 29

### 🚇 08:00 Osaka to Kyoto to Ginkakuji
📝 90 min to Ginkakuji. Train Osaka to Kyoto Station then Bus #100 or #5 to Ginkakuji-mae. Depart Osaka 8:00 AM.

### 📍 08:30 Ginkakuji (Silver Pavilion)
📍 2 Ginkakujicho, Sakyo-ku, Kyoto
📝 Opens 8:30 AM. Arrive early to avoid crowds. Dry sand garden. Tour pavilion before Philosophers Path.

### 📍 Philosophers Path (Tetsugaku-no-Michi)
📍 Tetsugaku no Michi, Sakyo-ku, Kyoto
📝 35-45 min walk south along cherry tree-lined canal. Optional stop: Honen-in (quiet temple off the path).

### 📍 Nanzenji Temple
📍 Nanzenji Fukuchicho, Sakyo-ku, Kyoto
📝 Via Eikando Temple. Massive Sanmon gate and Meiji-period brick aqueduct.

### 📍 Heian Shrine
📍 Okazakicho, Sakyo-ku, Kyoto
📝 10-15 min walk west from Nanzenji. Massive red torii gate. Stroll through shrine gardens.

### 📍 Maruyama Park + Yasaka Shrine
📍 Maruyama, Higashiyama-ku, Kyoto
📝 15 min south toward Gion. Famous weeping cherry tree in Maruyama Park. Walk through park into Yasaka Shrine (center of Gion).

### 📍 Higashiyama Streets - Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka
📍 Higashiyama, Kyoto
📝 Traditional pedestrian stone streets with shops and cafes. Very crowded during sakura season - go early or late afternoon.

### 📍 Kiyomizudera Temple
📍 Kiyomizu, Higashiyama-ku, Kyoto
📝 10-15 min uphill from Sannenzaka. Iconic wooden stage with sunset views over Kyoto. End the day here.

# Kyoto — Points of Interest

## Entertainment

### Kyoto International Manga Museum / 京都国際マンガミュージアム
📍 452 Kinbukicho, Nakagyo-ku, Kyoto 604-0846
🏷️ madeline, anime, entertainment
👥 Crowd: Rarely crowded — good rainy day option. Extensive manga library you can read in the courtyard.
⏰ Best time: Open 10am. Allow 2-3 hours. Madeline layer: rotating exhibitions, large manga archive (~300,000 volumes), cosplay events. Adults ~900 yen.

## Neighborhood

### Higashiyama Streets (Ninenzaka/Sannenzaka)
📍 Higashiyama, Kyoto
🏷️ historic, shopping, traditional, pedestrian
👥 Crowd: Very crowded during sakura and autumn leaf seasons
⏰ Best time: Traditional stone pedestrian streets with shops and cafes. Leads up to Kiyomizudera.

## Park

### Maruyama Park
📍 Maruyama, Higashiyama-ku, Kyoto
🏷️ park, sakura, weeping_cherry, gion
👥 Crowd: Famous weeping cherry tree packed in sakura season
⏰ Best time: Famous weeping cherry tree. Gateway to Gion.

## Shrine

### Heian Shrine
📍 Okazakicho, Sakyo-ku, Kyoto
🏷️ shrine, torii, gardens, okazaki
⏰ Best time: Massive red torii gate. Okazaki area.

### Yasaka Shrine
📍 625 Gionmachi Kitagawa, Higashiyama-ku, Kyoto
🏷️ shrine, gion, lanterns, festivals
⏰ Best time: Center of Gion. Walk through from Maruyama Park.

## Sightseeing

### Arashiyama Bamboo Grove / 嵐山竹林
📍 Sagaogurayama Tabuchiyama-cho, Ukyo-ku, Kyoto
🏷️ group, sightseeing, early-morning, crowd-warning
👥 Crowd: The 5-minute bamboo path is tourist-overrun by 10am. At 7am you may have it nearly to yourself.
⏰ Best time: 7am. The grove path is short (5-10 min walk) — pair with Tenryu-ji garden (opens 8:30am, 500 yen) and Okochi Sanso villa. Monkeys on the hillside at Iwatayama park nearby.

### Fushimi Inari Taisha / 伏見稲荷大社
📍 68 Fukakusa Yabunouchi-cho, Fushimi-ku, Kyoto
🏷️ group, sightseeing, early-morning, crowd-warning
👥 Crowd: Gates never close. By 9am the base torii is overrun with photographers. The upper mountain trails stay manageable even at 8am — most tourists turn back at the first shrine plateau.
⏰ Best time: DAWN ONLY — 6am is otherworldly. The full trail to the summit takes 2+ hours; even just the first 30 min up is incredible before crowds. Accessible: Namba → Kintetsu to Tofukuji → JR to Inari (2 min walk to gates).

### Gion District (Hanamikoji-dori) / 祇園（花見小路通）
📍 Gion, Higashiyama-ku, Kyoto
🏷️ group, sightseeing, evening
👥 Crowd: Midday is the worst — packed with selfie sticks. Dusk (6-8pm) is when Gion is atmospheric and most likely to spot geiko/maiko walking to appointments.
⏰ Best time: DUSK — 6-8pm on Hanamikoji-dori. Do NOT block geiko or maiko — it is considered very rude and there are now signs prohibiting it. Just observe from the side of the street.

### Philosopher's Path (Tetsugaku-no-Michi) / 哲学の道
📍 Tetsugaku no Michi, Sakyo-ku, Kyoto
🏷️ group, sightseeing, cherry-blossom
👥 Crowd: Cherry blossom peak makes this one of Kyoto's most photographed spots. Morning is manageable; afternoon gets crowded.
⏰ Best time: Morning walk during cherry blossom season. 2km canal-side path flanked by ~500 cherry trees. Leads from Nanzen-ji to Ginkaku-ji (Silver Pavilion).

### Ginkakuji (Silver Pavilion)
📍 2 Ginkakujicho, Sakyo-ku, Kyoto
🏷️ silver_pavilion, zen, sand_garden, early
👥 Crowd: Opens 8:30 AM, arrive early to avoid crowds
⏰ Best time: Start here at opening. Tour pavilion and dry sand garden.

👥 Crowd: Quiet, off the main path
⏰ Best time: Quiet temple off Philosophers Path. Worth a short detour.

### Kiyomizudera Temple
📍 Kiyomizu, Higashiyama-ku, Kyoto
🏷️ temple, wooden_stage, views, sunset
⏰ Best time: Iconic wooden stage with panoramic views over Kyoto. Best at sunset.

### Nanzenji Temple
📍 Nanzenji Fukuchicho, Sakyo-ku, Kyoto
🏷️ temple, zen, aqueduct, sanmon
⏰ Best time: Massive Sanmon gate and Meiji brick aqueduct. End of Philosophers Path.

### Neighborhood

**Higashiyama Streets - Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka**
Traditional stone pedestrian streets in Higashiyama, Kyoto. Lined with shops and cafes in historic machiya buildings. Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka lead uphill to Kiyomizudera. Very crowded during sakura and autumn - go early or late afternoon.

**Maruyama Park - weeping cherry tree and Gion gateway**
Maruyama, Higashiyama-ku, Kyoto. Famous weeping cherry tree, packed during sakura season. Walk through park directly into Yasaka Shrine and the Gion district.
